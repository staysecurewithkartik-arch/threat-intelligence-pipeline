================================================================================

When asked: "Tell me about a project you've built"

30-SECOND VERSION:
"I built a threat intelligence pipeline that ingests firewall logs, email headers, and SIEM alerts, then uses Claude AI to extract IOCs, correlate them across sources, and generate risk-scored threat intelligence reports with MITRE ATT&CK mapping. It bridges my SOC experience with CTI skills."

2-MINUTE VERSION:
"In my SOC role, I analyze individual security alerts—firewall blocks, suspicious emails, SIEM detections. But the real intelligence work is connecting the dots: finding the same threat actor across multiple data sources.

I built a system that automates that. It ingests three types of data:
1. Firewall logs (what we're blocking)
2. Email headers (where attacks come from)
3. SIEM alerts (what happened inside the network)

The system extracts indicators—IPs, domains, hashes—and correlates them across sources. If the same IP appears in a firewall block AND an email header AND a SIEM alert, that's high confidence that it's a real threat.

I use Claude AI to analyze these patterns intelligently, map them to MITRE ATT&CK techniques to understand attacker tactics, score the risk on a 0-100 scale, and generate structured threat intelligence reports.

What this demonstrates:
- I understand threat intelligence workflows
- I can build automation tools
- I think strategically, not just operationally
- I'm ready for a CTI role now, not in 6 months"

FOLLOW-UP QUESTIONS YOU'LL GET:

Q: "Why Claude and not ChatGPT?"
A: "Claude handles unstructured security data better. It's good at analyzing context across multiple sources—a single IP in different contexts (C2 server vs just a blocked IP) means different things, and Claude understands that nuance."

Q: "How would you extend this?"
A: "Real-time integration with threat feeds (VirusTotal, AlienVault OTX), actor tracking over time, web dashboard for visualization, Slack alerts for high-risk incidents, and eventually integration with our SIEM for automated investigation kickoff."

Q: "What was hardest?"
A: "Getting Claude to consistently output structured JSON with reliable field values. I had to iterate on prompts to be very explicit about format and error handling for cases where the analysis doesn't fit perfect JSON."

Q: "How's this different from off-the-shelf tools?"
A: "Most commercial CTI platforms are black boxes or require extensive configuration. This is transparent, extensible, and specifically tailored to our threat landscape. Plus I built it to understand the CTI workflow, not just use someone else's tool."
